# Silent brand diagnostic reference

Derive these internally; explain conclusions in plain language.

- **Trust stakes:** what failure costs in money, health, time, reputation, effort, or disappointment.
- **Trust temperature:** how formal, warm, restrained, or playful the brand can credibly be.
- **Cultural anchor and scripts:** audience vocabulary, locality, language, and type-system implications.
- **Category flexibility:** `constrained | mixed | open`; name which codes carry trust and where differentiation is safe.
- **Archetype:** primary and optional secondary personality scaffold, never a formula.
- **Reference tribe:** qualities the brand should feel related to and explicitly unlike. Actual sites require live UI Research.
- **Distinctive assets:** `established | candidate | unresolved`; evaluate ownability, memorability, repetition, and cross-surface usefulness.
- **Refusals:** user-stated or strongly implied brand-specific boundaries with reasons.

Use category-entry and distinctive-asset research as heuristics. Conform/break ratios are discussion aids, not scientific rules. Do not cite confounded brand/business anecdotes as causal proof. Frequency and information density come from Foundation/STRUCTURE.
