# Plain-language brand diagnostic

Return a short narrative covering who the audience is, how they feel before and after, what failure costs, the credible personality and cultural vocabulary, what must feel familiar, where the brand can be distinctive, the candidate/protected memorable assets, and what it should refuse.

Give the recommended interpretation and invite correction. Do not expose axis labels as a questionnaire. A distinctive asset may remain candidate or unresolved. If the user disagrees, update the diagnosis; their explicit brand decision outranks the heuristic.
